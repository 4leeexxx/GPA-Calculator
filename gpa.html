How Is GPA Calculated?

GPA is calculated by multiplying each course's grade points by its credit hours, summing all results (called quality points), then dividing by the total credit hours. For example: an A (4.0) in a 3-credit course gives 12 quality points. A B (3.0) in a 4-credit course gives 12 quality points. Total: 24 quality points ÷ 7 credits = 3.43 GPA.

GPA Scale Reference

  • A / A+ = 4.0  ·  A− = 3.7
  • B+ = 3.3  ·  B = 3.0  ·  B− = 2.7
  • C+ = 2.3  ·  C = 2.0  ·  C− = 1.7
  • D+ = 1.3  ·  D = 1.0  ·  F = 0.0

Why Track Your GPA?

Your GPA affects scholarship eligibility, academic probation, college admissions, and job applications. Many scholarships require a minimum 3.0 GPA. Graduate school programs often require a 3.5 or higher. Knowing your GPA early in the semester gives you time to adjust your study strategy.
